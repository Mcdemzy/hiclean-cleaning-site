Cleaning Tips

Flour Cleaning, 01-Aug-2023

Flour can indeed be used as a natural and effective cleaning agent for certain tasks around the house. It's absorbent and abrasive properties make it useful for various cleaning purposes. To clean with flour, simply sprinkle a generous amount of flour on the surface you want to clean. Then, use a damp cloth to rub the flour into the surface. Flour can help remove tough stains and grime from stainless steel surfaces. Sprinkle some flour on the stainless steel and use a soft cloth to rub it in a circular motion. The flour will absorb grease and residue, leaving the surface clean and shiny.Flour can be used to polish silverware and remove tarnish. Mix flour with water to form a paste and apply it to the tarnished silver. Let it dry, then buff it off with a soft cloth to reveal the shine.

Glass Cleaning, 01-Aug-2023

Maintaining a sparkling clean glass seems to be a difficult task for most people. There are a few simple things you can do to keep your glass clean and looking its best.Opt for a commercial glass cleaner or create your own by mixing equal parts of water and white vinegar in a spray bottle. Avoid using harsh chemicals or ammonia-based cleaners, as they can leave streaks and harm the environment.Before spraying the glass cleaner, dust the glass surface with a soft microfiber cloth or a feather duster. This step will remove any loose dirt or debris, ensuring a smoother cleaning process.When wiping the glass, choose a lint-free cloth or microfiber towel to avoid leaving behind lint or fibers. Paper towels can also work, but they may not be as effective as microfiber in preventing streaks.

Carpet Cleaning, 01-Aug-2023

To keep your carpet clean, you need to vacuum it regularly. Vacuuming helps to remove dirt, dust, and debris from the carpet fibers, which can prevent stains from forming..... You should vacuum your carpet at least once a week, but more often if you have pets or young children. Address stains immediately to prevent them from setting in. Blot the stain with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb as much liquid as possible. Avoid rubbing, as it can spread the stain. Then, use a carpet stain remover or a homemade solution (such as a mix of water, white vinegar, and mild dish soap) to treat the stain gently. Before using any carpet cleaning solution or stain remover, test it on a small, inconspicuous area of the carpet to ensure it doesn't cause discoloration or damage.
